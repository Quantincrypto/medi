3 Approaches to Fix Your UX & CTA Flow

All three fix the form. They differ in how much of the page structure they touch.

A

Fix CTAs & Form Only

Targeted changes, no page restructuring. Shortest to implement.

  • Shorten lead form to 5 fields (Name, WhatsApp, Country, Equipment Type, Hospital Name)
  • Make "Request Pricing" the dominant hero CTA, "Browse Listings" secondary/ghost style
  • Product page gets an inline mini-form (no more redirect away)
  • Remove the duplicate floating WhatsApp button on mobile (keep only the sticky bar)
  • WhatsApp becomes secondary everywhere — smaller, grey-toned
LOW EFFORT ~5–8 files touched
B

Homepage Reorder + Fix CTAs ⭐ Recommended

Everything in A, plus restructure the homepage for a better buyer narrative.

  • Everything in Approach A
  • Move "How It Works" to right after the trust badge bar — buyers understand the process before browsing
  • Add a social proof placeholder strip (logos, "X hospitals trust us", quote cards)
  • Mobile: make "Request Pricing" the dominant sticky bar button, WhatsApp smaller
  • Stats strip gets better numbers (or reframe what's shown — e.g. "48h response" is more compelling than "3 continents")
MEDIUM EFFORT ~8–12 files touched BEST IMPACT/EFFORT RATIO
C

Full Funnel Redesign

Everything in B, plus capture intent at every stage of the browse journey.

  • Everything in Approach B
  • Category pages get a mini "Tell us what you need" prompt at the top
  • Country pages get a "Browse [Country] equipment + get a quote" CTA strip
  • Catalogue page gets a sticky sidebar enquiry panel on desktop
  • Exit-intent or scroll-depth triggered form nudge
HIGH EFFORT ~15+ files touched HIGHEST CEILING

My recommendation: B. It fixes every issue you selected, moves the page narrative into a logical buyer journey, and doesn't require touching every page on the site. You can always layer on C later once B is live and converting.

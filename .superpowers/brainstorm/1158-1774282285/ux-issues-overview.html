UX Audit: Key Issues Found

I've reviewed all your pages. Here are the biggest opportunities — click the ones you most want to fix.

1

Lead Form Has Too Much Friction

8 required fields before someone can even say "I'm interested" — most B2B buyers bounce on long forms at first contact. The form asks for budget, job title, brand preference, WhatsApp, hospital name all at once.

HIGH IMPACT Hero + Request Page
2

No Clear Primary CTA in the Hero

The hero has two equal-weight buttons ("Browse Verified Listings" + "View Suppliers") competing for attention. Users don't know what to do first. The lead form is also there on desktop — three things competing at once.

HIGH IMPACT Homepage
3

"How It Works" Is Buried Too Low

The 3-step process (Browse → Request Data Room → Secure Procurement) appears after categories, featured listings, and supplier cards. New visitors who don't understand the process leave before they scroll that far.

MEDIUM IMPACT Homepage Flow
4

Mobile Lead Form Is Hidden

On mobile, the lead form only appears at the very bottom of the page (after all sections). The sticky bottom bar has WhatsApp and "Request Pricing" as equal-weight buttons — but there's also a floating WhatsApp button above it, creating visual confusion.

MEDIUM IMPACT Mobile
5

No Social Proof or Testimonials

The site leads with "49+ listings, 6 markets, 3 continents" — these numbers feel small for a B2B authority site. There are no hospital logos, buyer testimonials, or case studies to build trust with a procurement manager who's never heard of you.

MEDIUM IMPACT Homepage + Product Pages
6

Product Page CTAs Send Users Away

On product pages, "Request Verified Data Room & Pricing" links to a separate page with the full form — breaking momentum. A buyer ready to enquire on a specific product should be able to do so right there, ideally with the product pre-filled.
